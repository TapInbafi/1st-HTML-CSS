Perceptions of Senior High School STEM Students on AI Chatbots as a Tutoring Platform for Science Education

ABSTRACT

This research assessed the perceptions of STEM students on AI chatbots as a tutoring resource for science education at Bloomingfields Academy Foundation Incorporated during the academic year 2024 - 2025. A quantitative questionnaire was utilized to gather data from 129 students regarding their perceptions of AI chatbots in the context of science learning. The findings suggest that AI chatbots have a positive impact on the learning experiences of students in science. Generally, students find chatbots helpful for grasping complex concepts, enhancing academic performance, and improving efficiency in completing science-related tasks. They are viewed as user-friendly and easy to use. However, while students hold a positive view of AI chatbots, their commitment to using them consistently varies. Additionally, trust in their reliability, security, and accuracy remains neutral, suggesting some hesitation in fully relying on them for academic support. Despite recognizing their potential benefits, actual usage patterns reflect a degree of indifference, highlighting a disconnect between perception and actual engagement. Future research should investigate the effectiveness of AI chatbots through experimental studies that compare academic performance between users and non-users. Additional studies should also look into enhancing AI chatbots with features like adaptive learning, personalized feedback, and real-time problem-solving capabilities. These insights could contribute to the development of more engaging and dependable AI tutoring tools for students, educators, and developers.

Keywords: AI chatbots, Science Education, STEM Students, Tutoring
